The surviving field notes suggest

World Champignons was not founded. It was discovered.

The first mention of the name appeared in a damp leather journal found beneath the floorboards of an abandoned mushroom cannery somewhere in the Pacific Northwest. The journal belonged to Mortimer “Moss” Wexley: failed botanist, part-time boxing coach, traveling soup inspector, and possibly liar.

He claimed he had stumbled upon a global order of fungal philosophers operating quietly beneath the civilized world. Nobody believed him. Which, according to the journal, was exactly the point.
